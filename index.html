Most Popular Application

Window Cleaning with Deionized Water

Professional window cleaners worldwide are switching to deionized water for one simple reason: it produces consistently streak-free, spot-free results without squeegees or chemical solutions.

When ordinary tap water evaporates, it leaves behind dissolved minerals — calcium, magnesium, silica — that form visible spots and streaks. Deionized water has had these minerals removed through ion exchange, so it evaporates cleanly every time.

  • Eliminates the need for squeegees and detergents
  • Works with water-fed pole systems for multi-story buildings
  • Reduces labor time by up to 50% on residential jobs
  • Leaves glass cleaner than traditional methods

Understanding The Science

How Does Deionized Water Clean So Well?

Ordinary tap water contains dissolved minerals — calcium, magnesium, iron, silica — measured as Total Dissolved Solids (TDS). When tap water evaporates on a surface, these minerals remain behind as visible spots, streaks, and film. Deionized water has been purified through an ion exchange process that removes virtually all dissolved minerals, resulting in water with a TDS reading near zero. When DI water evaporates, nothing is left behind — just a perfectly clean surface.

1

Ion Exchange Removes Minerals

Water passes through specialized resin beds that swap dissolved mineral ions for hydrogen and hydroxide ions, producing pure H₂O.

2

Pure Water Acts as a Solvent

With no minerals of its own, DI water actively absorbs dirt, grease, and contaminants from surfaces — it's hungry to dissolve things.

3

Clean Evaporation, Zero Residue

Because there are no dissolved solids, DI water evaporates completely clean — no spots, no streaks, no film.
