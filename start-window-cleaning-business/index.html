Start a Window Cleaning Business

The ultimate guide to launching a high-margin, professional window cleaning service using pure water technology.

The Professional Advantage

Starting a window cleaning business is one of the most accessible ways to enter the service industry. It offers low startup costs, recurring revenue, and immediate cash flow. However, the difference between a "guy with a ladder" and a professional service provider often comes down to technology.

By leveraging Pure Water Technology (deionized water + water-fed poles), you can clean faster, safer, and better than traditional methods. This technology allows you to reach windows up to three or four stories high while standing safely on the ground, all while producing a spot-free finish that traditional squeegee methods struggle to match.

Why Pure Water?

Traditional window cleaning relies on soaps and squeegees. This leaves a chemical film that actually attracts dust, leading to shorter intervals between cleanings. Deionized water is "hungry" water—it aggressively strips minerals and dirt from the glass and evaporates without leaving a single spot. Your customers' windows stay cleaner, longer.

Step-by-Step Launch Guide

Legal & Administration

Register your business entity (LLC or Sole Proprietorship), obtain a general business license, and most importantly, get general liability insurance. This protects you and your clients during on-site work.

Acquire Pure Water Equipment

Invest in a professional Water-Fed Pole (WFP) system. You'll need a telescopic pole, a specialized window cleaning brush, and a consistent source of deionized water. While some pros use RO/DI carts, many starters find that purchasing pre-deionized water in bulk is more cost-effective and reliable for high-quality results.

Marketing Your Services

Focus on local SEO and residential neighborhoods. Create a Google Business Profile, print professional door hangers, and offer "first-time customer" discounts. Highlight your "Pure Water, Chemical-Free" approach as a premium selling point for families and eco-conscious clients.

Pricing for Profit

Don't compete on price alone. Position yourself as a specialist. Most pros price by the window (e.g., $5-$10 per pane) or by the hour (targeting $60-$100+ per hour of labor). Because pure water cleaning is up to 50% faster than squeegee work, your effective hourly rate will be significantly higher.

Operational Success

Efficiency is the key to scaling. Use a CRM (Customer Relationship Management) tool to track your clients and schedule recurring maintenance (quarterly or bi-annual). Consistently testing your water purity with a TDS meter ensures that your "spot-free" promise remains true on every single job.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is window cleaning a profitable business?

Yes, window cleaning has very high profit margins, especially when using pure water technology which reduces labor time and eliminates the cost of chemicals.

Do I need a license to start window cleaning?

Requirements vary by state and city, but most areas require a general business license and liability insurance.

Why use DI water instead of traditional squeegees?

DI water is faster, safer (no ladders needed for 3 stories), and produces better results as it leaves no mineral residue on the glass or frames.

How much does it cost to start?

A basic professional setup can cost between $1,000 and $3,000. This covers a quality water-fed pole, initial DI water supply, and marketing materials.
